Welcome to the Van Gogh Collection, a website dedicated to showcasing all works of Van Gogh.

Here, you can get up close and personal with the world through Van Gogh's eyes, experiencing the stories and emotions behind those famous paintings.

“Be clearly aware of the stars and infinity on high.

Then life seems almost enchanted after all.”

(30 March 1853 – 29 July 1890)

Vincent Willem van Gogh, born on March 30, 1853, and passing away on July 29, 1890, was a renowned Dutch artist associated with the Post-Impressionist movement. His art, encompassing some of the most famous, beloved, and valuable paintings and drawings globally, remains profoundly influential.

Initially embarking on a career in art dealing, Van Gogh's early adulthood also saw him teaching and working as a missionary in a destitute mining area. It wasn't until 1880 that he pursued art full-time. Initially favoring dark palettes, his style transformed after encountering Impressionism and Neo-Impressionism in Paris, adopting their vibrant colors and techniques into a distinct style that came into its own during his stay in Arles, France. In the decade before his death, Van Gogh was prolific, creating over 2,000 works, including about 900 paintings and 1,100 drawings and sketches. His most celebrated pieces were made in the tumultuous final two years of his life, a period marked by the severing of his left ear amid a deteriorating relationship with Paul Gauguin, and subsequent episodes of mental illness that culminated in his suicide.

Theo, his brother, played a pivotal role in Vincent's life, providing unwavering financial and emotional support. The profound bond between the brothers is immortalized in the extensive correspondence they shared starting in August 1872. Van Gogh is heralded as a precursor to Expressionism, leaving a lasting legacy on 20th-century art, influencing movements such as Fauvism and German Expressionism.
